### **3. Understanding the Tactics**
#### **Acoustic & Sensory Harassment**
*   **What it is:** The use of low-frequency noise (infrasound), high-pitched frequencies, or "sound-masking" to cause sleep deprivation, headaches, and psychological distress.
*   **The Goal:** To induce a state of constant "fight or flight" and make the victim appear "unstable" to others when they try to explain the noise.

#### **Narcissistic Tech Abuse**
*   **Stalkerware:** Hidden apps that monitor every text, call, and GPS movement.
*   **Triangulation:** Using smart home devices or social media to create a "digital cage," where the abuser controls the victim's environment remotely.
*   **Hostile Surveillance:** The use of covert cameras and "pings" to let the victim know they are always being watched, eroding the sense of private sanctuary.

### **4. Targeted Discrediting: The "Perfect Victim" Trap**
*This section highlights how specific identities are weaponized to silence victims.*

*   **Gay Men & LGBTQ+ Individuals:**
    *   **The Tactic:** Threatening to "out" individuals in professional or family circles.
    *   **The Discrediting:** Using homophobic tropes to frame the victim as "promiscuous" or "unreliable" to delegitimize their claims of abuse.
*   **Recreational Drug Users & Those in Recovery:**
    *   **The Tactic:** Using surveillance to document past or current substance use.
    *   **The Discrediting:** Labeling the victim a "drug addict" or "unstable" so that authorities and family members dismiss their reports of tech harassment as hallucinations or "drug-induced paranoia."
*   **Victims of Sexual Abuse:**
    *   **The Tactic:** "Sextortion" and the threat of leaking intimate images.
    *   **The Discrediting:** Re-traumatization through digital stalking, making the victim feel that no space is safe, which often leads to social withdrawal that the abuser then frames as "unstable behavior."

### **5. For the Support Network (Families, Friends, Colleagues)**
*   **Believe the Narrative:** If a loved one mentions "strange noises" or "being watched," do not dismiss them. Tech abuse is designed to sound like "paranoia" to the untrained ear.
*   **Notice the Isolation:** Watch for colleagues or friends who suddenly withdraw from social circles or stop using their phones.
*   **Document, Don't Confront:** If you suspect tech abuse, do not confront the abuser. This can lead to an escalation in surveillance. Help the victim document evidence on a *safe, uncompromised device*.
*   **Address the Stigma:** Refuse to let an individual's drug history or sexual orientation be used as a reason to ignore their safety.

---

### **6. Safety Checklist (Call to Action)**
1.  **Use a Safe Device:** Access this site and help services from a public library or a friend’s phone.
2.  **Audit Your Home:** Look for "smart" devices you didn't install or unusual behavior in your Wi-Fi (e.g., unexpected devices connected).
3.  **Document Everything:** Keep a log of acoustic incidents (dates, times, physical symptoms) and tech glitches. Save screenshots to a secure cloud account the abuser doesn't know about.
4.  **Secure Your Accounts:** Change passwords on a "clean" device and enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) via an app, not SMS.
